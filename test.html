About Me

I'm Nguyễn Minh Hiếu, a computational physicist specializing in molecular structure simulations, specifically focused on peptide research and energy optimization using techniques like Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Neural Network Potentials (NNP). I graduated from Hanoi University of Science in Physics and have been actively working in research, particularly on low-energy conformers of peptides.

Research

My research focuses on applying DFT, DFTB, and machine learning approaches like NNP to search for stable low-energy conformations of tripeptides. Some of the key projects I've worked on include:

  • Optimizing peptide structures using NNP to predict stable conformers.
  • Energy minimization and stability analysis of peptide chains using quantum chemical methods.
  • Exploring the cis-trans isomerism in tripeptides and its effect on molecular energy levels.
